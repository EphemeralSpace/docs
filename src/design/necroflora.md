# Necroflora

{{#template ../templates/unimplemented.md}}

Necroflora is a invasive plant colony which can rapidly infect a unsuspecting ship, station, or planet. It slowly spreads over tiles as it grows over them, converting common objects into dangerous obstacles. As a radiotrophic organism, the radstorm is amplifying its growth. As the radstorm approaches closer, the Necroflora's spread, speed and potency increase.

# The Necroflora Threat

Necroflora is a map component suited for larger, 32 player maps. It aims to act as a more interesting obstacle to be used in map design and an actively progressing threat in rounds.
During the midround, stagehands will have the opportunity to vote to make the Necroflora into a much more impactful threat to the crew and enemy organizations alike.
For Map Design, Necroflora is a way to make parts of the map more interesting and threatening to traverse, adding a cost to exploring deep maints locations and navigating to key utilities or high grade loot zones. It slows down the process of looting maints, making deep maints loot no longer "first come, first served".
For Roundflow, Necroflora personifies the threat of the radstorm by itself becoming more dangerous as the radstorm approaches. Working to keep it contained adds another reactive crew task which requires more engaged coordination and intervention, while also rewarding crew for proactively organizing expeditions deep into Necroflora territory.
The design of the Floral Organs is intended to encourage working as a team to clear rooms out, and planning the method of clearing rooms carefully. Traversing into creep without a plan should feel like "bullet hell". You can dodge and weave, rewarding high skill, but one slip up can be very dangerous.
The Necroflora interacts with all three vectors of station decay in unique ways. Filth nourishes the Necroflora, speeding its growth up greatly when growing into a Filth filled area. Floral Creep generates water vapor when it meets fluids, creating the conditions for rust. Fire, on the other hand, is anathema to the highly flammable Necroflora, but wildly setting it on fire in the hopes of getting rid of it can have an equally detrimental effect to the station as having let it spread - even more detrimental if the Necroflora mutates.
Keeping the Necroflora contained shouldn't be too difficult for the crew. Welding doors makes the process of Necroflora forcing a door open much slower, at the cost of denying access to the room. Necroflora breaks down barricades on doors before it can attempt to pull open the door itself, meaning that a attentive crew member can quickly build a new barricade to prevent the door being breached.
Combating and fully defeating the Necroflora should require the combined talents and resources of the majority of the station. Medical making chemical bombs and curing caustic burns, engineering breaking down entry points or scrubbing pollen, botany using their weed killer supplies, the janitor cleaning up filth so it can't accelerate growth, the captain blasting Organs to pieces with their gun, maintenance working to repair the damage.


# Floral Creep

The foundation of Necroflora is the Floral Creep, a carpet which grows across floor tiles and can eventually overrun a whole station. Objects adjacent to or on top of Floral Creep may slowly degrade as the creep works its way through the station. Doors will be slowly forced open. Unattended lockers might become hosts to Floral Organs. Water tanks, jerrycans of fuel and blood spills are all greedily consumed
and converted into water vapor while accelerating creep growth. Standing on Floral Creep isn't slowing or damaging, but its presence is a sign of danger.
Necroflora can't spread through walls or closed doors. Crew can take advantage of this to contain and limit its spread.
Floral Creep and Floral Organs are immune to cold, suffocation and radiation, being adapted to survive in space. It can, however, be destroyed. Slashing it to pieces suffices, but fire, high temperatures and caustic chemicals can rapidly clear it from a room.


# Floral Organs

Certain suitable objects left unattended in a room with creep slowly become hosts for Floral Organs. Floral Organs typically serve as defense mechanisms for the creep, but some merely cause inconveniences for unprepared crew. Without exception, all Floral Organs are immobile.
- Lockers and Crates transform into Traplashes if there are none nearby, which shoot out vines as soon as they detect movement and pull whatever they hit inside them, locking shut. They can be tricked by putting another item in front of you, and will hold whatever they grab inside them for at least a minute. While in the case of getting caught you can break out from the inside, anything stuck inside will take damage over time.
- Vendors transform into Seedspewers, shooting out explosive seed pods whenever someone is standing on Floral Creep nearby.
- Gas Canisters transform into Pollensacs, constantly filling the room with mildly caustic pollen.
- Microwaves, Blenders, Faxes and other generic electronics transform into Thornnodes, which connect to nearby Thornnodes as well as other power drawing devices like APCs and Computers with thorny electrified vines.
- Corpses transform into Corpseblooms, shooting caustic pollen globs in a circular pattern at short intervals.
- Vents transform into Ventblossoms, which clog the vent, preventing air from being pumped in or out, and attack anyone moving nearby with damaging pollen glob projectiles.
Destroying a Floral Organ will leave the object it transformed from in a damaged but repairable state.

# Floral Events

While Floral Creep is on a station, the following events can appear as long as the round timer is past 30 minuites.
- Floral Incursion: Floral Creep begins to expand through the ventilation system, growing adjacent to vents at a chosen location.
- Floral Haze: All Ventblossoms and Pollensacs immediately produce one time burst of pollen, and Ventblossoms start pumping Pollen into the atmospheric network for the rest of the round.
- Tearing Vines: Walls near to Floral Creep begin being damaged for a short duration.
- Thermophile Mutation: The Necroflora becomes immune to fire, consuming it to accelerate growth, but loses its cold immunity.
